Recycled Plastics

Recycling is a complicated system dictated by market demand, price determinations, local regulations, the success of which is contingent upon everyone. “Recycling” is determined by two really important things: the market and city government. Moreover, any plastic material with food residues on (or in) it cannot be recycled. Therefore, recycling is not a easy thing and it is impossible to recycle every piece of plastic we use. As statistics showed, only 9% of the plastic waste is recycled. We have to reduce the use of plastic and find other alternatives.

Biodegradable Plastics

Biodegradable plastic is one kinds of bioplastic which is produced from renewable biomass sources, such as vegetable fats and oils, corn starch, recycled food waste, etc. Most biodegradable plastics serve as a middle ground between oil-based and plant-based plastics. They often combine these two types of resin in order to create plastic that breaks down quickly while still maintaining the properties of traditional plastic. Polylactic Acid (PLA) is the most commercially available biodegradable plastic which obtained from the fermentation of corn starch. Generally speaking, biodegradable plastic need about 5 years to be decomposed.

LEARN MORE

Compostable Plastics

Compostable Plastics are plant-based alternatives to traditional oil-based plastics. It is also one kinds of bioplastic which is produced from renewable biomass sources, such as vegetable fats and oils, corn starch, recycled food waste, etc. They are quick to biodegrade. Items like plates, bowls, and cups may take as little as one month to compost while other items such as clear containers and heat resistant utensils can take up to six months. Moreover, many of them are not waterproof.
